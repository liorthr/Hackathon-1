History

Origin and Foundations

The first man to recognize God is Abraham. His descendants are Itshak then Yaakov then the 12 tribes that will form the "Bnei Israel" a Jewish people named "the Hebrews"

Diaspora and Modern History:

The Jewish diaspora spans centuries, marked by dispersion across the globe. Facing challenges like the Inquisition and the Holocaust, it also saw cultural and political revival in modern times.

Israel



The land of Israel is the land destined for the Jewish people since the gift of the Torah (book of Jewry). God promised this land for the chosen people.

Rules

Shabbes

Shabbat is the weekly day of rest in Judaism, observed from Friday evening to Saturday evening, dedicated to prayer, rest, and spiritual reflection.

Cacherout (Kosher)

Jewish dietary laws define kosher foods that are permissible for consumption according to the Torah, regulating how food should be prepared, consumed, and obtained.

Tikkoun Olam

This concept emphasizes individuals' responsibility to contribute to improving and repairing the world through righteous actions and kindness towards others

Study Torah

The study of the Torah is central in Judaism, encouraging continuous learning of sacred texts to deepen spiritual and moral understanding.

613 rules

Mitzvot are divine commandments guiding the practices and behavior of Jewish adherents. They cover a wide range of subjects, from worship to charity, interpersonal relationships, and social justice.
